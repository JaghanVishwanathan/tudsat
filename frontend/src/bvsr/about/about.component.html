Budesverband Studentischer Raumfahrt (BVSR)

What is BVSR?

BVSR (Bundesverband studentischer Raumfahrt e.V.) is the German federation of student-led aerospace associations.
Founded in 2021, BVSR unites more than 15 university groups across Germany to:
  • Promote aerospace education and innovation.
  • Encourage collaboration across student projects.
  • Represent student interests in research, industry, and policy discussions

A Brief History

The idea for BVSR was born at the 2018 International Astronautical Congress in Bremen, and the federation was formally established in 2021. ,
Since then, the BVSR Conference has grown into the central meeting point of Germany’s student spaceflight scene

BVSR Conference

The BVSR Conference, first held in Munich in 2022, is a premier event for students and professionals in the space sector.
It offers members of BVSR associations the opportunity to attend engaging workshops and lectures, while students can network with peers and industry representatives,
fostering collaboration across student associations.
A key highlight of the conference is the Student Space Forum, which showcases the achievements of BVSR associations to students and the wider public.
Presentations from start-ups at the ESA Business Incubation Center and other industry companies provide further insights into the space sector.
The conference celebrates the accomplishments of students in space research and encourages participation in space-focused associations,
helping to strengthen the next generation of talent in the field.

BVSR Conference 2026

From May 14–17, 2026, the BVSR Conference returns to its home city.
Over four days, students, researchers, and industry representatives will gather for:
  • Workshops on spacecraft design, propulsion, and project management.
  • Keynotes and Panels from leaders in academia and industry.
  • Student Space Forum where projects are presented and discussed.
  • Networking Sessions to foster collaboration between universities and companies.
  • Social Events to connect the community in a more informal setting.
